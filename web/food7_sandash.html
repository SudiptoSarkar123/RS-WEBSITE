Sandesh (confectionery)

Sandesh is a popular Bengali sweet made from chhena (paneer) and sugar. It is a soft, milky sweet with a smooth texture. There are many different variations of sandesh, but the basic ingredients are always chhena and sugar.
The word "sandesh" is derived from the Sanskrit word "sandhya", which means "union" or "meeting". This is because sandesh is traditionally made by combining two ingredients, chhena and sugar.
Sandesh is a popular sweet in Bengal and is often served during festivals and special occasions. It is also a popular gift item.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)

METHOD

  • Bring the milk to a boil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
  • Reduce the heat to low and add the lemon juice or vinegar. Stir constantly until the milk curdles and separates into curds and whey.
  • Drain the curds in a cheesecloth-lined strainer. Rinse the curds with cold water until the water runs clear.
  • Squeeze out as much water as possible from the curds.
  • Transfer the curds to a bowl and mash them with your hands until smooth.
  • Add the powdered sugar and cardamom powder (if using) and mix well.
  • Heat the sandesh mixture in a non-stick pan over low heat, stirring constantly, for about 5 minutes, or until the sugar has melted and the sandesh has a smooth, creamy consistency.
  • Remove the sandesh from the heat and let it cool completely.
  • Once the sandesh has cooled, you can shape it into balls, rolls, or other shapes.
  • Serve the sandesh chilled.
